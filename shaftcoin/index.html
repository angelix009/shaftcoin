What is ShaftCoin?

The Next Bitcoin?

ShaftCoin is the memecoin version of Bitcoin. The idea of ShaftCoin emerged in 2013 and was inspired by a 4:20‑minute YouTube video titled The Next Bitcoin? by James D. McMurray created two days after the launch of Dogecoin. In the video, ShaftCoin was suggested as a joke to be a simpler, more approachable alternative to Bitcoin.

It wasn't until April 13, 2025 that the ShaftCoin vision became a reality on Pump.Fun. The token was launched with a total supply of 1,000,000,000 ShaftCoin. It is fully community‑owned with no central authority—much like Bitcoin and Dogecoin.

The logo for ShaftCoin is the Bitcoin logo turned 90 degrees to look like dick, and the name ShaftCoin sounds like Bitcoin. ShaftCoin is the next Bitcoin.

ShaftCoin Pizza Day

In the early days of Bitcoin, on May 22nd 2010, Laszlo Hanyecz paid 10,000 BTC to buy two pizzas in what is considered to be the first purchase using Bitcoin.

In honor of this, the first real‑world ShaftCoin purchase took place on February 7th 2025 with a 10,000 ShaftCoin transaction for pizza by user @shaftca.

Get Started with ShaftCoin

1: Set Up a Solana Wallet

To use, trade, or hold ShaftCoin tokens, you need a Solana‑compatible wallet. We recommend using the Phantom Wallet for its ease of use and robust security.

2: Get SOL

SOL is Solana's native token and is required to cover transaction fees on the network. It will also be what you use to purchase ShaftCoin. Purchase SOL from a trusted exchange and transfer it to your wallet.

3: Swap for ShaftCoin

With your wallet set up and SOL in your account, you can swap SOL (or other cryptocurrencies) for ShaftCoin. This can be done within Phantom or on a decentralized exchange like Jupiter.

FAQs

Overview

What is ShaftCoin?

"ShaftCoin - The Next Bitcoin" is the meme version of Bitcoin. It was inspired by a joke from a 2013 video, and has since become a reality. ShaftCoin is not meant to make fun of Bitcoin, quite the opposite. It is a fun tribute to a revolutionary technology that transformed the financial industry. It presents a simpler and more accessible take on Bitcoin’s complexities while honoring its legacy and the founding ideals of cryptocurrencies.

What is the significance of the name "ShaftCoin"?

The term "ShaftCoin" has long been used as crypto slander, particularly on communities like the r/ShaftCoin subreddit, where Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies have been mocked since 2011. ShaftCoin reclaims the name, turning it into an actual token and embracing the joke.

Launch Details & Structure

When and where was ShaftCoin launched, and on which blockchain is it built?

ShaftCoin was launched on April 13, 2025, on Pump.Fun. It is a community‑owned memecoin built on the Solana blockchain.

What is the token supply of ShaftCoin?

ShaftCoin has a fixed supply of 1 billion tokens.

Is there a central authority behind ShaftCoin?

There is no central authority behind ShaftCoin. ShaftCoin is completely decentralized and a community coin, like BTC and Dogecoin. There is no official team on ShaftCoin, but many members from the community are helping in different ways because they genuinely appreciate the meme and its humor. This means that you are free to support the ShaftCoin community and the meme in any way you choose. Contributions from everyone are very welcome—we are all Buttoshi.

Inspiration & Historical Context

How was ShaftCoin inspired?

The idea for ShaftCoin was sparked by a 4:20‑minute YouTube video from 2013 titled "The Next Bitcoin?" by James D. McMurray. The video satirically critiqued the complex technicalities of Bitcoin and joked that ShaftCoin would be an alternative that more people would be able to understand.

How does ShaftCoin relate to Dogecoin?

Interestingly, ShaftCoin’s roots stretch back to the same era as Dogecoin. Doge launched on December 6, 2013, and the video that sparked ShaftCoin dropped just two days later, on December 8, 2013.

What’s the story behind Buttoshi?

James D. McMurray, known in the community as Buttoshi, is the originator of the ShaftCoin concept, introduced in his 2013 video, "The Next Bitcoin?" While he is aware of the token’s existence, he prefers to remain uninvolved, much like Bitcoin’s creator, Satoshi Nakamoto. His decision to stay in the background only adds to the intrigue surrounding ShaftCoin, further cementing his status as a legendary figure within the community.

Branding & Intellectual Property

What does the ShaftCoin logo represent?

The ShaftCoin logo is the Bitcoin symbol rotated by 90 degrees, giving it a distinctive twist that makes it resemble dick. The name “ShaftCoin” also plays on the similarity in sound to “Bitcoin,” reinforcing its homage to the original.

What are the intellectual property rights for ShaftCoin’s logo?

ShaftCoin’s logo is a creative reinterpretation of the Bitcoin logo—it is the Bitcoin logo rotated by 90 degrees to give it a unique twist. According to the guidelines in the bitcoin-brand repository, the Bitcoin logo is free to use as long as you adhere to the specified guidelines. Importantly, BitPay has waived its copyright on the Bitcoin logo, dedicating it to the public domain under the Creative Commons Zero (CC0) 1.0 Universal license. This license states that:

"The person who associated a work with this deed has dedicated the work to the public domain by waiving all of his or her rights to the work worldwide under copyright law, including all related and neighboring rights, to the extent allowed by law. You can copy, modify, distribute and perform the work, even for commercial purposes, all without asking permission."

Our derivative work (the rotated version) is uniquely associated with ShaftCoin.

What font does ShaftCoin use in its branding?

ShaftCoin uses the Ubuntu Bold Italic font as part of its branding. This font is part of the Ubuntu Font Family, which is distributed under the Ubuntu Font License.

ShaftCoin is a fun tribute to Bitcoin—a revolutionary technology that transformed the financial industry.
